# SCV

Reference image: `liquipedia-scv.jpg`

The reference reads as a compact industrial worker viewed from an isometric angle, but do not let the bottom machinery and perspective shadows drive the design. For our sprite, use the same simple machine-infantry language as Marine: square cabin/head, two side blocks, and rectangular arms. We do not see a separate body; the head/cabin covers that read.

Distinctive features:

- Square cabin/head.
- Two square side blocks.
- Two rectangular arms that angle outward in a V shape.
- Small worker/tool read without drawing individual hands.

SVG plan:

- `panels`: two rectangular arm bars angled out in a V, then two square side blocks, then square cabin/head on top.
- `lines`: optional tiny tool seam at the arm tips.
- `cores`: optional cabin light.
- Keep it wider than a Marine and shorter than a Vulture.

Omit:

- Bottom legs/feet implied by the isometric view, hoses, tread clutter, face detail, tiny mechanical clutter, shadow, and underside.
